5. Define Ranula. What are the clinical features? How do you treat the same?

Answer:
Ranula is a mucocele of the sublingual gland. Clinical features include a blueish swelling in the floor of the mouth. Treatment options include marsupialization or excision...

Answer:
Surgical audit is a systematic review of surgical performance. Steps include defining objectives, data collection, analysis, and implementing changes...

8. Enumerate clinical features and management of malignant melanoma.

Answer:
Clinical features include asymmetry, border irregularities, color variations, diameter >6mm, and evolution. Management involves surgical excision and possible immunotherapy.

9. Enumerate clinical features and management of papillary thyroid carcinoma.

Answer:
Clinical features include a palpable thyroid nodule, and management typically involves thyroidectomy.

10. Write in detail about types, clinical features and management of tetanus.

Answer:
Tetanus is caused by Clostridium tetani. Types include generalized, localized, and neonatal. Clinical features include muscle stiffness, spasms, and lockjaw. Management involves antitoxin administration, wound care, and supportive therapy...
