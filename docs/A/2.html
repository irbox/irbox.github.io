LONG ESSAYS (2 x 10 = 20 Marks)

1. Discuss the etiology, clinical features, and management of thromboembolism.

Answer:
Thromboembolism is caused by various factors, including hypercoagulable states, venous stasis, and endothelial injury. Clinical features may include pain, swelling, and redness in the affected area, while management involves anticoagulation therapy, thrombolysis, or surgical intervention in severe cases.

2. Explain the etiology, pathogenesis, clinical features, diagnosis, and management of tuberculous lymphadenitis.

Answer:
Tuberculous lymphadenitis is caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. The pathogenesis involves formation of caseating granulomas. Clinical features often include painless lymphadenopathy, systemic symptoms like fever and night sweats. Diagnosis is via fine needle aspiration cytology or biopsy, and management typically involves a prolonged course of antitubercular therapy.

SHORT ESSAYS (3 x 5 = 15 Marks)

3. Define rodent ulcer.

Answer:
Rodent ulcer, also known as basal cell carcinoma, is a type of skin cancer that typically appears as a small, raised lesion with a waxy appearance. It often occurs on sun-exposed areas of the skin.

4. Describe electrolyte abnormalities in congenital hypertrophic pyloric stenosis.

Answer:
Patients with congenital hypertrophic pyloric stenosis frequently present with hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis due to vomiting of gastric contents. Dehydration may also lead to hypokalemia.

5. What is cystic hygroma?

Answer:
Cystic hygroma is a congenital malformation of the lymphatic system, resulting in a fluid-filled sac, typically located in the neck or axilla. It is often associated with chromosomal abnormalities.

SHORT ANSWERS (5 x 3 = 15 Marks)

6. What is plunging ranula?

Answer:
Plunging ranula is a type of salivary gland cyst that extends from the oral cavity into the neck, usually resulting from the rupture of a sublingual ranula.

7. Define speckled leukoplakia.

Answer:
Speckled leukoplakia is a clinical term for a white patch with red components seen in oral mucosa, which may represent dysplasia and is associated with an increased risk of malignancy.

8. What is a sternomastoid tumor?

Answer:
A sternomastoid tumor, also known as torticollis, is a condition where the sternocleidomastoid muscle is shortened or fibrosed, leading to head tilt and neck deformity in infants.

9. Explain cancrum oris.

Answer:
Cancrum oris, also known as noma, is a severe gangrenous infection of the face, often associated with malnutrition, poor hygiene, and underlying diseases, leading to tissue necrosis.

10. Describe Adson’s test.

Answer:
Adson’s test is a clinical maneuver used to assess for thoracic outlet syndrome. It involves the patient turning their head towards the affected side while the physician palpates the radial pulse, looking for its diminishment.
