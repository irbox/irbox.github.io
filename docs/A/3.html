LONG ESSAYS 2 x 10 = 20 Marks

1. Describe the symptoms, signs investigations and treatment of varicose veins.

Answer: Varicose veins are characterized by swollen, twisted veins that often appear blue or dark purple. Symptoms may include aching, heavy legs, swelling, and visible veins. Signs include dilated and tortuous veins. Investigations may involve Doppler ultrasound. Treatment includes lifestyle changes, compression stockings, sclerotherapy, and surgical options like vein stripping.

2. Define and classify shock. How will you manage a case of Hypovolemic shock?

Answer: Shock is a life-threatening condition where there is inadequate blood flow to meet the metabolic needs of tissues. It can be classified as hypovolemic, cardiogenic, distributive, or obstructive. Management of hypovolemic shock involves intravenous fluid resuscitation, administration of blood products if necessary, and addressing the underlying cause such as controlling bleeding.

SHORT ESSAYS 3 x 5 = 15 Marks

3. Blood transfusion, its indications and complications.

Answer: Blood transfusion is the process of transferring blood components into a patient's circulation. Indications include severe anemia, massive hemorrhage, and thrombocytopenia. Complications may include allergic reactions, hemolytic reactions, and transfusion-related acute lung injury (TRALI).

4. Mixed parotid tumour.

Answer: Mixed parotid tumors, or pleomorphic adenomas, are benign tumors that can arise from the salivary glands. They present as a painless, slow-growing mass. Treatment involves surgical excision, and the prognosis is generally good, although recurrence is possible.

5. Keloid.

Answer: Keloids are raised scars that develop due to abnormal healing responses following skin injury. They are characterized by excessive collagen production and can be itchy or painful. Treatment options include intralesional corticosteroids, silicone gel sheets, and surgical excision.

SHORT ANSWERS 5 x 3 = 15 Marks

6. Phylloides tumour.

Answer: Phylloides tumor is a rare breast tumor that can be benign or malignant. It is characterized by rapid growth and can appear as a lump in the breast. Surgical excision is the main treatment and is essential to ensure negative margins.

7. Thyroglossal cyst.

Answer: A thyroglossal cyst is a congenital condition that results from the remnants of the thyroglossal duct. It typically presents as a midline neck swelling, especially noticeable when the patient protrudes their tongue. The definitive treatment is surgical excision.

8. Gas gangrene.

Answer: Gas gangrene is a severe soft tissue infection caused primarily by Clostridium species. It is characterized by rapid progression of tissue necrosis, pain, and gas formation. Treatment includes surgical debridement, antibiotics, and supportive care.

9. Rule of nine in Burns.

Answer: The Rule of Nines is a method used to estimate the total body surface area affected by burns. In adults, the body is divided into sections, each representing approximately 9% (or multiples thereof) of total body area.

10. Berry aneurysm.

Answer: A berry aneurysm is a spherical dilation of a blood vessel at a bifurcation in the brain, resembling a berry. They are commonly associated with polycystic kidney disease and can lead to subarachnoid hemorrhage if ruptured. Surgical clipping or endovascular coiling is often indicated for treatment.
