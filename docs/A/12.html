LONG ESSAYS (2x1) - 20 Marks

1. A 45-year old lady presents with a painless lump of 2x2 cm size to start within the right breast and has doubled the size in 6 months.

Answer:
1) What other history is needed?
Answer: Past medical history, family history of breast cancer, menstrual history, and any history of previous breast disease or surgery.
2) What is your differential diagnosis?
Answer: Fibroadenoma, breast carcinoma, cyst, phyllodes tumor, and lipoma.
3) How do you investigate?
Answer: Breast ultrasound, mammography, and, if necessary, biopsy.
4) Add a note on management of early breast carcinoma.
Answer: Surgical excision, possibly lumpectomy followed by radiotherapy, and consideration of hormonal therapy if receptor-positive.

2. A 40-year old lady presents with swelling in the front of the neck of 10 years duration, gradually increasing in size.

Answer:
1) List the differential diagnosis.
Answer: Multinodular goitre, thyroid carcinoma, cystic lesions, and lymphadenopathy.
2) Symptoms of hyperthyroidism.
Answer: Weight loss, heat intolerance, palpitations, tremors, and increased appetite.
3) Complications of multinodular goitre.
Answer: Compression of surrounding structures, malignant transformation, and hyperthyroidism.
4) Investigations for multinodular goitre.
Answer: Thyroid function tests, ultrasound, fine needle aspiration biopsy, and thyroid scan if indicated.

SHORT ESSAYS

3. Enumerate types of shock and principles of resuscitation.

Answer:
Types of shock include hypovolemic, cardiogenic, distributive (including septic), and obstructive shock. Resuscitation principles involve fluid replacement, restoration of hemodynamic stability, and addressing the underlying cause.

4. Advantages and disadvantages of minimally invasive surgery.

Answer:
Advantages: Less postoperative pain, shorter recovery time, and minimal scarring. Disadvantages: Limited tactile feedback, risk of injury to surrounding organs, and the need for specialized training.

5. Enumerate clinical features and write a note on management of parathyroid adenoma.

Answer:
Clinical features include hypercalcemia symptoms such as kidney stones, osteoporosis, and abdominal pain. Management involves surgical removal of the adenoma, monitoring calcium levels, and addressing associated symptoms.

6. Enumerate clinical features and write a note on management of tension pneumothorax.

Answer:
Clinical features include respiratory distress, hypoxia, hypotension, tracheal deviation, and decreased breath sounds on the affected side. Management involves immediate decompression (needle thoracostomy) and definitive treatment with chest tube insertion.

7. Principles of laparoscopy.

Answer:
Principles include the use of carbon dioxide for pneumoperitoneum, visualization with laparoscope, and minimally invasive techniques to limit patient trauma and promote quicker recovery.

8. Clinical features and management of pleomorphic adenoma.

Answer:
Clinical features include a painless, slow-growing mass in the parotid gland. Management typically involves surgical excision, ensuring complete removal to prevent recurrence.

9. Investigations of a surgical patient.

Answer:
Investigations may include laboratory tests (CBC, liver function tests, renal function tests), imaging studies (X-ray, ultrasound, CT), and specific tests based on the clinical condition.

10. Complications of blood transfusion.

Answer:
Complications may include febrile reactions, allergic reactions, hemolytic reactions, transfusion-related acute lung injury (TRALI), and infections.

SHORT ANSWERS

11. Factors affecting wound healing.

Answer:
Factors include blood supply, oxygenation, infection, foreign body presence, age, nutritional status, and the use of medications like steroids.

12. Clinical manifestation of Multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1.

Answer:
Clinical manifestations include hyperparathyroidism, pancreatic endocrine tumors, and pituitary adenomas.

13. Glasgow coma scale in head injuries.

Answer:
The Glasgow Coma Scale assesses patients based on eye, verbal, and motor responses, with a total score ranging from 3 to 15 indicating level of consciousness.

14. Principles of organ transplantation.

Answer:
Principles include donor selection, matching donor and recipient, immunosuppression to prevent rejection, and lifelong monitoring of the recipient.

15. Ethics in general surgery.

Answer:
Ethical considerations involve informed consent, balancing risks and benefits, patient autonomy, and confidentiality.

16. Indications for daycare surgery.

Answer:
Indications include minor procedures, patient health status allowing for quick recovery, and procedures not requiring extensive postoperative care.

17. Enumerate clinical features of cystic hygroma.

Answer:
Clinical features include soft, compressible masses usually located in the neck or axilla, and potential respiratory distress if the mass compresses airways.

18. How do you arrive at a diagnosis of squamous cell carcinoma of skin?

Answer:
Diagnosis is made through clinical examination, biopsy, and histopathological analysis showing atypical keratinocytes.

19. Immunological basis of transplantation.

Answer:
The immunological basis involves recognizing major histocompatibility complex (MHC) molecules and the role of T-cells in graft rejection and acceptance.

20. Parkland formula for burns.

Answer:
The Parkland formula estimates fluid resuscitation volume for burn patients as 4 mL × % total body surface area burned × body weight (kg), administered over 24 hours.
