LONG ESSAYS 2 x 10 = 20 Marks

1. Define Shock. Enumerate classification of shock. Discuss clinical features and management of septic shock.

Answer: Shock is a life-threatening condition characterized by inadequate blood flow to meet the metabolic demands of tissues. Classifications of shock include hypovolemic, cardiogenic, distributive (including septic), and obstructive shock. Clinical features of septic shock include hypotension, fever, altered mental status, and increased respiratory rate. Management involves fluid resuscitation, administration of antibiotics, and supportive care for organ function.

2. Enumerate cause of peripheral vascular disease. Discuss clinical features, investigations and management of a case of Buerger’s disease.

Answer: Causes of peripheral vascular disease include atherosclerosis, thrombosis, and embolism. Buerger’s disease, also known as thromboangiitis obliterans, is associated with smoking. Clinical features include pain in the limbs, coldness, and ulcers. Investigations may involve Doppler ultrasound and angiography. Management focuses on smoking cessation, symptomatic treatment, and, in severe cases, surgical intervention.

SHORT ESSAYS 3 x 5 = 15 Marks

3. Complications of blood transfusion

Answer: Complications of blood transfusion can include allergic reactions, hemolytic reactions, infections, and transfusion-related acute lung injury (TRALI). Monitoring and proper blood typing are essential to minimize risks.

4. Basal cell carcinoma

Answer: Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) is a type of skin cancer arising from basal cells. It is often caused by sun exposure. Clinically, it presents as a pearly nodule or ulceration. Treatment typically involves surgical excision or Mohs micrographic surgery.

5. Deep vein thrombosis

Answer: Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) occurs when a blood clot forms in a deep vein, typically in the legs. Risk factors include immobility and certain medical conditions. Clinical features may include swelling, pain, and redness in the affected limb. Management includes anticoagulant therapy and, in some cases, thrombolysis.

SHORT ANSWERS 5 x 3 = 15 Marks

6. Pilonidal sinus

Answer: A pilonidal sinus is a small hole or cavity in the skin, typically located near the tailbone. It may become infected and require surgical treatment.

7. Leukoplakia

Answer: Leukoplakia is a condition characterized by white patches in the oral cavity, often due to irritation or tobacco use. It requires monitoring for potential malignant transformation.

8. Sebaceous cyst

Answer: A sebaceous cyst is a closed sac under the skin filled with a thick, cheese-like substance. They are generally harmless but may require excision if symptomatic.

9. Ranula

Answer: A ranula is a mucocele that occurs in the floor of the mouth, typically due to the obstruction of a salivary gland. Surgical intervention may be needed for persistent or symptomatic cases.

10. Desmoid tumour

Answer: A desmoid tumor is a benign fibrous growth that can occur in various anatomical locations. While they can be locally aggressive, they do not metastasize. Treatment may involve surgical removal and close observation.
