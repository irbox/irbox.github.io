LONG ESSAYS (2 x 10 = 20 Marks)

1. Define shock, describe the pathophysiology of shock, types and principles of management of shock.

Answer:
Shock is a life-threatening condition characterized by inadequate perfusion of tissues, leading to cellular dysfunction. The pathophysiology involves decreased blood flow, oxygen delivery, and tissue hypoxia. Types include hypovolemic, cardiogenic, distributive, and obstructive shock. Management principles involve restoration of blood volume, stabilization of hemodynamics, and addressing underlying causes.

2. Describe the etiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and principles of management of arterial occlusive disease.

Answer:
Arterial occlusive disease is caused by atherosclerosis, thrombosis, or embolism. Clinical features include intermittent claudication, rest pain, and ulceration. Investigations include Doppler ultrasound, angiography, and CT angiography. Management principles involve lifestyle changes, medication, angioplasty, or surgical revascularization as necessary.

SHORT ESSAYS (3 x 5 = 15 Marks)

3. Difference between hypertrophic scar and keloid.

Answer:
Hypertrophic scars are raised scars that do not extend beyond the original wound boundaries, often improving over time. Keloids, on the other hand, are more prominent, extend beyond the original wound, and may continue to grow over time.

4. Imperforate anus.

Answer:
Imperforate anus is a congenital condition characterized by the absence of an anal opening. It can lead to serious complications like fecal obstruction and requires surgical intervention to create an anal passage.

5. ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography).

Answer:
ERCP is a diagnostic and therapeutic procedure used to visualize the bile ducts and pancreatic duct. It involves endoscopic access to the duodenum, cannulation of the ampulla, and injection of contrast dye for imaging, and can also treat conditions like choledocholithiasis.

SHORT ANSWERS (5 x 3 = 15 Marks)

6. Gas gangrene.

Answer:
Gas gangrene is a severe, rapidly progressing infection caused by anaerobic bacteria, typically Clostridium species, leading to tissue necrosis and gas production. Immediate surgical intervention and antibiotics are critical for management.

7. Acute cystitis.

Answer:
Acute cystitis is an infection of the bladder characterized by dysuria, frequency, urgency, and suprapubic pain. Diagnosis is often made through urinalysis and culture, with treatment primarily involving antibiotics.

8. Propofol.

Answer:
Propofol is a short-acting intravenous anesthetic agent used for induction and maintenance of anesthesia and sedation. It is known for its rapid onset and recovery properties.

9. Disinfectant.

Answer:
A disinfectant is a chemical substance used to eliminate or reduce harmful microorganisms on surfaces to prevent infection. Common disinfectants include bleach, alcohols, and quaternary ammonium compounds.

10. Stem cells.

Answer:
Stem cells are undifferentiated cells with the potential to develop into various cell types in the body. They are crucial for development, growth, and repair and hold potential for regenerative medicine and therapy for various diseases.
