LONG ESSAYS 2 x 10 = 20 Marks

1. Discuss the etiology, clinical features, investigations and treatment of primary Thyrotoxicosts.

Answer:
Thyrotoxicosis is mainly caused by conditions such as Graves' disease, toxic multinodular goiter, and thyroiditis. Clinical features include weight loss, palpitations, heat intolerance, and nervousness. Investigations include serum thyroid hormone levels (T3, T4), TSH levels, and uptake studies. Treatment may involve antithyroid medications, radioactive iodine, or surgery depending on the underlying cause and severity.

2. Discuss the differential diagnosis of carcinoma of breast. Describe management of stage II Breast.

Answer:
Differential diagnosis includes benign breast conditions such as fibroadenoma, cysts, and infections. Stage II breast cancer management involves multimodal treatment, including surgery (mastectomy or lumpectomy), adjuvant chemotherapy, and possibly radiation therapy, depending on tumor characteristics and lymph node involvement.

SHORT ESSAYS 3 x 5 = 15 Marks

3. Basal cell carcinoma

Answer:
Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) is the most common skin cancer, characterized by slow growth and a tendency to occur in sun-exposed areas. Treatment options include surgical excision, Mohs micrographic surgery, and topical therapies. Early diagnosis results in excellent prognosis.

4. Papillary carcinoma of thyroid

Answer:
Papillary thyroid carcinoma is the most prevalent form of thyroid cancer, often arising in younger patients. It is generally treated by surgical removal of the thyroid (thyroidectomy) and may require radioactive iodine therapy depending on the stage and spread of the disease.

5. Gynaecomastia

Answer:
Gynaecomastia is the enlargement of breast tissue in males, often due to hormonal imbalances, medication side effects, or systemic diseases. Management includes treating the underlying cause, and surgery (subcutaneous mastectomy) may be considered for significant or persistent cases.

SHORT ANSWERS 5 x 3 = 15 Marks

6. Tension Pneumothorax

Answer:
Tension pneumothorax is a life-threatening condition where air enters the pleural space and cannot escape, leading to mediastinal shift and compromised respiratory and cardiovascular function. Immediate needle decompression and subsequent chest tube placement are required.

7. Dermoid cyst

Answer:
Dermoid cysts are benign tumors containing various tissue types, including skin, hair, and sometimes teeth. They are commonly found in the ovaries but can occur in other areas. Surgical excision is the standard treatment if symptomatic or for cosmetic reasons.

8. Secondary haemorrhage

Answer:
Secondary hemorrhage occurs days to weeks after a surgical procedure due to the erosion of blood vessels or infection leading to vessel disruption. Management involves identifying the source and may require surgical intervention to control bleeding.

9. Intermittent claudication

Answer:
Intermittent claudication is a condition characterized by muscle pain or cramping during physical activity due to inadequate blood flow. Management includes lifestyle modifications, exercise therapy, medications to improve circulation, or surgical revascularization if severe.

10. Leucoplakia

Answer:
Leucoplakia refers to white patches on mucosal surfaces that cannot be scraped off. It is often associated with tobacco use and may signify precancerous changes. Treatment typically involves eliminating risk factors, monitoring, and possible biopsy to evaluate for dysplasia or malignancy.
