LONG ESSAYS (2 x 10 = 20 Marks)

  1. Describe the clinical features and management of Wilm’s tumour
    Answer: The clinical features of Wilm’s tumour include abdominal swelling, hematuria, and hypertension. Management involves surgical resection followed by chemotherapy, with the specific regimen tailored to the stage and histology of the disease.
  2. Describe etiology, complications and management of chronic duodenal ulcer
    Answer: The etiology of chronic duodenal ulcers includes Helicobacter pylori infection and the use of NSAIDs. Complications can include bleeding, perforation, and gastric outlet obstruction. Management includes proton pump inhibitors, antibiotics for H. pylori, and possibly surgical intervention.

SHORT ESSAYS (3 x 5 = 15 Marks)

  1. Surgical Anatomy of liver
    Answer: The liver is anatomically divided into two main lobes, the right lobe and the left lobe, separated by the falciform ligament. Key structures include the hepatic veins, hepatic artery, and bile ducts.
  2. Pheochromocytoma
    Answer: Pheochromocytoma is a tumor of the adrenal gland that secretes catecholamines, leading to symptoms like hypertension, headaches, and palpitations. Management typically involves surgical removal and preoperative stabilization with alpha-blockers.
  3. Classify parotid tumours
    Answer: Parotid tumors can be classified into benign conditions like pleomorphic adenoma and malignant tumors such as mucoepidermoid carcinoma and adenoid cystic carcinoma. Diagnosis often involves imaging and biopsy.

SHORT ANSWERS (5 x 3 = 15 Marks)

  1. Tracheostomy - indications
    Answer: Indications for tracheostomy include prolonged mechanical ventilation, airway obstruction, and the need for secretions management.
  2. Indications for cholecystectomy in asymptomatic gallstone disease
    Answer: Indications include the presence of large gallstones, gallbladder polyps larger than 1 cm, and evidence of diabetes or other comorbidities that could lead to complications.
  3. Thyroglossal cyst
    Answer: A thyroglossal cyst is a fibrous tissue remnant from the embryonic descent of the thyroid gland. It typically presents as a midline neck mass that moves with swallowing.
  4. Complications of hernia
    Answer: Complications of hernia include incarceration, strangulation, and bowel obstruction, which can lead to serious morbidity if not addressed promptly.
  5. Types of fibroadenoma of breast
    Answer: Fibroadenomas can be classified as simple or complex. Simple fibroadenomas have a uniform structure, while complex fibroadenomas contain other changes like cysts or epithelial hyperplasia.
