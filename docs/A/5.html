LONG ESSAYS (2 x 10 = 20 Marks)

1. Classify salivary tumours. Describe the etiology, pathogenesis, clinical features, and management of pleomorphic adenoma.

Answer: Salivary tumors can be classified into benign and malignant types. The most common benign tumor is pleomorphic adenoma, which typically presents as a painless swelling. Etiology includes genetic factors and environmental influences. The pathogenesis involves mixed epithelial and mesenchymal elements. Clinical features include a firm, mobile mass, and management typically involves surgical excision.

2. Describe the etiopathogenesis, clinical features, prophylaxis, and management of tetanus.

Answer: Tetanus is caused by the bacterium Clostridium tetani, which produces a neurotoxin. The etiopathogenesis involves contamination of wounds with spores. Clinical features include muscle stiffness and spasms. Prophylaxis includes vaccination with tetanus toxoid. Management involves administering tetanus immunoglobulin, antibiotics, and supportive care.

SHORT ESSAYS (3 x 5 = 15 Marks)

3. Cleft lip.

Answer: Cleft lip is a congenital deformity characterized by a split or opening in the upper lip. It can occur alone or with cleft palate. Surgical repair is recommended, and rehabilitation includes speech therapy and dental care.

4. Ameloblastoma.

Answer: Ameloblastoma is a benign but locally aggressive odontogenic tumor. It typically presents as a painless swelling of the jaw. Management predominantly involves surgical resection to prevent recurrence.

5. Fluid replacement in burns.

Answer: Fluid replacement in burns is critical for maintaining hemodynamic stability. The Parkland formula is commonly used, recommending 4 mL/kg/%TBSA (total body surface area burned) administered over the first 24 hours. Close monitoring of urine output is essential in the management.

SHORT ANSWERS

6. Electrical burns.

Answer: Electrical burns occur due to direct contact with an electrical source. They can cause deep tissue damage and arrhythmias. Immediate assessment and management are crucial for survival.

7. Metabolic acidosis.

Answer: Metabolic acidosis is characterized by a decrease in blood pH due to increased acid production or decreased bicarbonate levels. Causes include renal failure, diabetic ketoacidosis, and lactic acidosis. Management focuses on correcting the underlying cause and may involve bicarbonate therapy.

8. Marjolin’s ulcer.

Answer: Marjolin’s ulcer is a type of squamous cell carcinoma that arises in chronic wounds or scars. It is associated with poor prognosis and requires wide surgical excision and possible reconstruction.

9. Duplex scan.

Answer: A duplex scan is a non-invasive imaging technique that uses ultrasound to visualize blood flow and structures within blood vessels. It is commonly used to diagnose vascular conditions such as thrombosis and stenosis.

10. Collar stud abscess.

Answer: Collar stud abscess is a localized infection often associated with irritation from jewelry or attire. Treatment involves incision and drainage, along with appropriate antibiotics to manage infection.
