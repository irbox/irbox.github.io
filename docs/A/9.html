LONG ESSAYS 2 x 10 = 20 Marks

  1. Describe the etiology, pathogenesis, clinical features and management of carcinoma tongue.
    Answer: The etiology of carcinoma of the tongue includes risk factors such as tobacco use, alcohol consumption, and HPV infection. Pathogenesis involves genetic mutations leading to uncontrolled cell growth. Clinical features may include ulceration, pain, and dysphagia. Management consists of surgical resection, radiotherapy, or chemotherapy based on the stage of the disease.
  2. What is a wound? Describe the phases of wound healing and the factors affecting wound healing.
    Answer: A wound is a break in the skin or mucous membrane. The phases of wound healing include hemostasis, inflammation, proliferation, and remodeling. Factors affecting wound healing include age, nutritional status, oxygen supply, and presence of infection.

SHORT ESSAYS: 3 x 5 = 15 Marks

  1. Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome.
    Answer: Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS) is a clinical syndrome characterized by two or more of the following: Fever or hypothermia, tachycardia, tachypnea, and leukocytosis or leukopenia. It indicates an inflammatory response that can be triggered by infection, trauma, or other insults to the body.
  2. Indications of Blood transfusion and its complications.
    Answer: Indications for blood transfusion include anemia, severe blood loss, and certain medical conditions like hemophilia. Complications may include allergic reactions, febrile non-hemolytic transfusion reactions, and transfusion-related acute lung injury (TRALI).
  3. Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma.
    Answer: Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma is a diverse group of blood cancers that includes any lymphoma except Hodgkin's lymphomas. It is characterized by the proliferation of lymphocytes and can present with swollen lymph nodes, fever, weight loss, and night sweats.

SHORT ANSWERS 5 x 3 = 15 Marks

  1. Split skin grafting.
    Answer: Split skin grafting is a surgical technique where a thin layer of skin is removed from a donor site and placed over a wound or burn, aiding in its healing and closure.
  2. Ankle-Brachial Pressure Index.
    Answer: The Ankle-Brachial Pressure Index (ABPI) is a simple test that compares the blood pressure in your ankle with the blood pressure in your arm to assess blood flow and diagnose peripheral arterial disease.
  3. Fresh Frozen Plasma.
    Answer: Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP) is a blood product composed of liquid portion of blood that is frozen and used to treat patients with clotting disorders, as it contains clotting factors and proteins.
  4. Hypokalemia.
    Answer: Hypokalemia is a condition characterized by low levels of potassium in the blood, which can lead to symptoms such as muscle weakness, irregular heart rhythms, and cramps.
  5. Virchow's Triad.
    Answer: Virchow's Triad is a set of three factors that increase the risk of thrombosis: venous stasis, vascular injury, and hypercoagulability.
